Built Like a Home, Not a Shed.

A high-quality garden room is one of the most cost-effective and least disruptive ways to add valuable square footage to your property. Unlike traditional extensions, they rarely require planning permission and can be constructed in a fraction of the time.

However, at Tom Cutts Joinery & Building, we refuse to install cheap, flat-pack kits. Our bespoke garden rooms are timber-framed from the ground up and engineered to modern housing standards.

We utilize multi-layer foil insulation, breathable membranes, robust EPDM rubber roofing, and premium external cladding to ensure your space remains a comfortable, dry, and secure environment 365 days a year.

The Anatomy of a Tom Cutts Garden Room

A stunning exterior is worthless if the structure beneath rots or freezes. We bring our 18 years of commercial and domestic building expertise to every single timber we lay.

  • Heavy-Duty Foundations

    We assess ground conditions to provide the correct base—whether that's a solid concrete slab or environmentally friendly ground screws—ensuring zero subsidence.

  • Triple-Insulated Framework

    Using thick structural timber, we pack the cavities with rigid foil-backed PIR board (such as Celotex) across the floors, walls, and ceiling to create an impenetrable thermal envelope.

  • Premium Exterior Cladding

    Choose from contemporary Western Red Cedar, Siberian Larch, or zero-maintenance composite slatted cladding for a striking architectural finish.

  • EPDM Rubber Roofing

    Say goodbye to tearing roofing felt. We use single-piece Firestone EPDM rubber roofs boasting a 50+ year lifespan and complete watertight protection.

Garden Room FAQs

In the majority of cases, garden rooms fall under 'Permitted Development' rights and do not require formal planning permission, provided they meet certain height and boundary regulations. We conduct a full site survey to ensure your design complies before building commences. If planning is required, we can guide you through the process.
